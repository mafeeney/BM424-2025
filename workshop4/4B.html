At the previous emergency pandemic response team meeting, the following action points were agreed:

  • Testing and halting the sale of contaminated bird products

  • Increase awareness of the importance of personal hygiene, hand washing, hand sanitisers etc and have resources (e.g. posters and leaflets) available in public spaces for educating public

  • Providing rapid and at home influenza antigen-dependent immunological diagnostic assay testing

  • vaccination program against H9N2 for all potentially affected bird species

  • Making antiviral medications (oseltamivir and zanamivir) more accessible to all positive patients by supplying them in pharmacies and allowing prescription after evidence of a positive at-home diagnostic test

Pandemic Report

Briefing:

  • Based on collated hospital reports and death certificates, it is believed that there have been 37 deaths due to influenza in Baltyre thus far

  • There are currently 432 patients in the Queen’s College with confirmed influenza, or symptoms consistent with influenza

  • There are currently 9 patients currently in the ICU with confirmed influenza

  • 251 home testing kits have been distributed to the public

  • A vaccination program has been set up; 62 have been distributed to date

  • Pharmacies have rolled out distribution of anti-viral drugs, with 497 prescriptions to date

  • A company has been contracted to begin a public awareness campaign

  • Testing of potentially contaminated bird products has begun; a sample of the results is shown in Table 1.

Your committee has also been monitoring news and published blog posts about the pandemic in Baltyre, in order to better gauge public opinion. A representative sample of these is shown below.

News/blog post #1

Increasing Concerns Over Outbreak of influenza in Baltyre
Baltyre Chronicle

The situation appears to be quite grim in Baltyre, as we see cases mount and the city struggles under the wave of another pandemic, coming hard on the heels of the COVID pandemic which began nearly five years ago.

To date there have been 37 deaths attributed to the pathogen, Alphainfluenzavirus influenzae, and hundreds of people are currently ill with the disease.

The city’s Emergency Pandemic Response Committee seems to be struggling to pin down the source of the outbreak. Microbiological tests have confirmed that the city’s water cooling towers appear to be free of Alphainfluenzavirus influenzae, and rigorous inspections (threatening hefty fines) have suggested that companies are already following the water safety guidelines that have been established to prevent influenza.

The Emergency Pandemic Response Committee was not available for comment. The First Minister of Scotland made a statement saying that every effort possible is being made to tackle the pandemic in Baltyre.

News/blog post #5

BBC Scotland
influenza pandemic in Baltyre
by Thomas Ray
09 March, 2025

Hard on the heels of the COVID-19 pandemic and a number of other health scares (monkeypox, or “mpox”, avian flu, and worries about other infectious disease outbreaks), another pandemic has hit the Scottish town of Baltyre. This time, it’s influenza, the same virus that has caused global pandemics in the past.

There have been 37 deaths in the pandemic so far, but perhaps the most frightening aspect to this new pandemic is rumours that this variant of avian influenza has crossed the species barrier and can be transmitted from human to human.

Scientists have been talking about this possibility for years, warning of the potential for a massive pandemic similar to the 1918 “Spanish Flu”.

Is the virus circulating in Baltyre the one, or is the next influenza pandemic still around the corner?
